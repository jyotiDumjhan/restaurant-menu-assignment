Phở
$8.99

Pho is pure Vietnamese comfort: silky rice noodles in a fragrant beef broth simmered with star anise, cinnamon, charred onion, and ginger. Topped with fresh bean sprouts, Thai basil, cilantro, and a squeeze of lime, it’s finished with tender beef slivers—and a dash of hoisin and Sriracha for your perfect sweet-and-spicy twist.

(Who needs a spa day when you’ve got a bowl this soothing?)

Bánh Mì
$6.50

Bánh mì is a crunchy French-style baguette filled with creamy pâté and mayo, layered with savory grilled pork (or your choice of ham, chicken, or tofu), pickled carrots and daikon for zing, crisp cucumber slices, and fresh cilantro. A dash of soy sauce or chili garlic adds that perfect salty-spicy kick—every bite is a harmony of textures and flavors.

(Bread: the only carb guaranteed to bring joy—and crumbs everywhere.)

Gỏi Cuốn
$7.50

Gỏi cuốn are fresh Vietnamese spring rolls: delicate rice-paper wraps packed with tender shrimp (or pork), cool vermicelli noodles, crisp lettuce, crunchy herbs like mint and cilantro, plus a hint of crunchy bean sprouts. Dip each roll into a creamy peanut-hoisin sauce—spiked with lime and chili—for a light, refreshing bite that’s pure summer in every mouthful.

(Define “guilt-free”? Just call this “spring roll.”)

Momo
$5.99

Momo are pillowy Nepali dumplings: tender steamed dough pillows stuffed with spiced minced meat (often yak, chicken, or veggies), garlic, ginger, and cilantro. Served hot with a tangy tomato‐chil­i achar and cool yogurt dip, they’re a cozy, flavor‐packed bite in every mouthful.

(Because life is too short for sad snacks.)

Dal Bhat
$9.50

Dal bhat is Nepal’s soul food: fluffy steamed rice paired with a creamy, spiced lentil soup (dal), plus a lineup of sautéed greens, tangy pickles, and savory vegetable or meat curries—each spoonful bringing hearty, earthy warmth and bright bursts of flavor.

(Eating lentils since 500 BC—look who’s trendy now.)

Sel Roti
$4.50

Newari cuisine is Kathmandu Valley’s festive feast: crispy lentil pancakes (bara), savory rice crepes (chatamari), smoky chili-garlic meats (choila), sweet rice dumplings (yomari), plus tangy pickles and fermented greens—an unforgettable blend of textures and spices in every bite.

(Sweet rings that won’t judge your midnight cravings.)
